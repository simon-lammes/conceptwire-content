What should you be aware of when falling into the water?
Watch approaching riders

You should see whether oncoming riders clearly see you and are able to drive around you with a safe distance. If a rider seems to accidentally ride directly towards you, you might be able to prevent injuries by putting your head below water and covering it with your arms.

Watch approaching handles

When you cannot avoid an approaching handle, you should put your head underwater. This can safely prevent the handle from damaging your face - especially your nose.

Only swim where you are clearly visible to approaching drivers

Swimming behind obstacles can lead to others not seeing you and unsuspectingly driving over the obstacle - leaving them potentially not enough time to avoid driving into you.

Leave the water fast

This rule is especially important in the starting area because other riders might potentially have to wait for the starting area to clear.
